Sport Overview

Associaton football is an invasion sport, commonaly called football or soccer depending on the country. It is played on a field approximantley 105m x 68m, with 11 players on the pitch for each team.

Positions

There are 4 main position groups for the 11 players on a football pitch: goalkeeper, defense, midfeild, and forward. Each team has 1 goalkeeper on the field at a time, they wear a seperate color from the rest of their team and are allowed to use their hands within the goalkeeping box. Teams will typically have between 3-5 defenders on the field at a time. Their main job is to prevent the oppossing team from scoring. Midfielder are the most versitile position, and have multiple subclasses including cetral, attacking, defensive, and left/right wing. They are generally involved in both attcking and defending. Forwards main goal is to score goals for their team. Depending on the lineup wings can be classified as midfielders or forwards.

Professional Play

Professional football is played world-wide, with the men's game boasting some of the highest payed athletes in the world and a growing women's side. International football is goverend by the Federation internationale de Football Association (FIFA), with each continent having its own confederation: the Asian Football confederation (AFC), Confederation of African Football (CAF), Confederation of North, Cenral American and Caribbean Association Football (CONCACAF), Confederacion Sudamericana de Futbol (CONMEBOL), Oceania Football Confederation (OFC), and Union of European Football Associations (UEFA). FIFA holds a World Cup evey 4 years (consecutivley) for the men's and women's game, with 2022 and 2023 respectively being the next ones. The Asian Cup, UEFA championships, and OFC Nations Cup are held every 4 years. While the Africa Cup of Nations, CONCACAF Gold Cup are held every 2 years and Copa America held approximantley every 3 years. For many of the Women's championships they follow the same schedule as their respective men's cup but some vary. Also to note is the unlike in the men's game where the U-20 teams compete at the Olympics, the senior Women's national teams compete instead, making the Olympics major competition for women's international football but not men's.

Women's Leagues

Women's football is still a growing sport, with major leagues just now starting to grow past infancy. The largest leagues are primarily located in Europe with the Women's Super League (WSL) in England, La Liga Feminina in Spain, Division 1 Feminine in France, and Frauen-Budesliga in Germany. The leading teams in these competitions also take part in the Women's Champion's League, along with teams from other leagues in Europe. The National Womens Soccer League (NWSL) in the United States was also historically the strongest league, and still boasts some of the largest average crowds in Women's football. The Liga MX Femenil in Mexico, W League in Australia and WE League in Japan are also some notable leauges; all proped up by the internationally competive national teams.
