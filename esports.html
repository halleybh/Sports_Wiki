Biggest Games in Esports

MOBAs

MOBA stands for Massive Online Battle Arena, and are games generally played on PC with 5 players on each team. Players work as a team to level up their characters and buy items through defeating minons and destroying enemy players. The first well know MOBA was a MOD (motification) for Warcraft III called Defense fo the Ancients (DotA).

Dota2

The Dota Pro Circuit was set up in 2011, and has grow in a league that spans the globe. Each season spans 1 calander year, with 3 tours in a season. The regional leagues function in round-robin tournaments, with teams competing to qualify for the season's majors (elimination-style tournaments). The 6 global regions are North America (NA), South America (SA), Western Europe (WEU), Eastern Europe (EEU), China (CN), and Southeast Asia (SEA). At the end of each season, after the conclusion of the 3 tours, there is a major intertiaonl tournament. [Ref: https://www.dota2.com/esports/ti11/about]


League of Legends

League of Legends, owned by Riot games was released in 2009 and has had a major pro scene since 2013. Each season is over the course of a calander year and split in 2 splits, with a Mid-Seasonal Invitaitonal (MSI) between them and the World Championship (Worlds) after the 2nd split. For MSI each region recives one qualifying spot and for Worlds the amount of qualify spots varies by region strength, with the major regions holding the majority of the them. There are 4 major regions: LCS (NA), LEC (Europe), LPL (China), and LCK (Korea) along with multiple minor regions who also recieve slots at the world championships: PCS (Southeast Aisa), VCS (Vietnam), OCE (Ocenia), LJL (Japan), TCL (Turkey), CBLOL (Brazil), LCL (Russia?), and LLA (Latin America).
